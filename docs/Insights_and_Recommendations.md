# Insights & Recommendations

## Dashboard Insights

**On burden & prevalence**
- Across 2000–2024 the dashboard counts **690M+** people living with HIV (cumulative). Rising prevalence does **not** indicate worsening infections — better ART keeps patients alive, so HIV is increasingly a **manageable chronic condition**. Higher prevalence can actually reflect healthcare success. *(In the most recent year, ~35.2M people are living with HIV.)*
- **African countries carry the highest HIV burden** — high infection rates, large numbers living with HIV, and significant mortality — reflecting how healthcare inequality, poverty and limited treatment access continue to affect outcomes across the region.
- Comparing infection trends with death trends shows that in many countries **death rates are falling faster than infection rates** — evidence that healthcare systems and HIV interventions are improving survival through better access to medication and long-term care.

**On treatment & implementation**
- Countries with **stronger ART coverage** generally show lower mortality, better survival outcomes and improved long-term disease management. As treatment access improves, mortality tends to fall.
- Yet some countries record **high "Deaths per 1,000 New Infections"** even where coverage looks moderate or high — pointing to weak healthcare delivery, late diagnosis, poor patient follow-up, medication interruptions or data-reporting inconsistencies.
- **Treatment availability alone is not enough** if implementation systems are weak — strong delivery, monitoring and reporting are as critical as access.

## Recommendations

**1 · Expand access, prevention & early diagnosis**
Reducing the global HIV burden requires early diagnosis, improved treatment access, stronger healthcare systems and effective prevention. Governments and healthcare organizations should expand community testing, awareness campaigns and mobile screening services to encourage early detection and reduce transmission. Increasing access to affordable antiretroviral therapy (ART) — particularly in high-burden regions such as Sub-Saharan Africa — is essential to lowering HIV-related deaths and improving quality of life.

**2 · Strengthen systems, monitoring & data**
Healthcare systems must strengthen patient monitoring, treatment distribution and data-reporting processes to ensure consistent care and informed decision-making. Preventive education, condom distribution and youth-awareness initiatives remain critical to reducing new infections. Leveraging tools such as Microsoft Power BI helps organizations monitor HIV trends, identify high-risk populations and support evidence-based policymaking. Overall, sustainable progress depends on integrating prevention, treatment, accurate reporting and data-driven healthcare strategies.

### Priority actions (data-driven)
1. **Close the coverage gaps** — fund low-ART, high-burden countries first (Pakistan, Indonesia, Madagascar, Congo, Sudan).
2. **Re-energise prevention** — renew PrEP, testing and education as new infections plateau.
3. **Fund the growing care pool** — sustainable, long-term financing for lifelong ART.
4. **Target retention hotspots** — Lesotho, Ethiopia, Malawi, Zimbabwe (diagnosis, adherence, viral suppression).
5. **Modernise data reporting** — move five-yearly indicators to annual reporting everywhere.
